# Moyin Lean Canvas

## @Overview

A high-level synthesis of Project Moyin's business logic, mapped to the Lean Canvas framework for an Industrial AI Interactive Engine.

---

### 1-2. Problems & Customer Segments

| 1. Problem                                                                                                   | 2. Customer Segments                                                                                            |
| :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **High Production Costs**: Traditional animation costs $50k/min, making it unreachable for indie developers. | **Visual Novel Gamers**: Players who value deep, narrative-driven content and are willing to pay for unique IP. |
| **Long Lead Times**: Months or years to produce, failing to adapt to fast-paced Web3/TikTok trends.          | **Anime Consumers**: Heavy users of short-form video who demand frequent, episodic entertainment.               |
| **Hollow AI Content**: Existing AI animation is often static or lacks narrative depth.                       | **Early Adopters**: Users who are tech-positive and embrace AI-assisted indie creative tools.                   |

---

### 3-4. UVP & Solution

| 3. Unique Value Proposition (UVP)                                                                                             | 4. Solution                                                                                |
| :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Tokyo Legal Strategy**: Strategically leveraging Japanese copyright laws for clear, high-compliance AI training frameworks. | **Moyin Engine**: An industrial-grade production pipeline built on ComfyUI and Rust logic. |
| **Alive Character Experience**: Automated, emotional performances via Director Agents vs. static, soulless graphics.          | **Industrial Efficacy**: Targets 2 weekly episodes (20 mins/week) at a cost < $15/min.     |

---

### 5. Channels

- **TikTok / Shorts**: Top-of-funnel viral content for user acquisition.
- **YouTube / Bilibili**: Content archiving and mid-funnel community retention.
- **Steam / DLsite**: High-value product distribution and final monetization.

---

### 6-7. Revenue & Cost Structure

| 6. Revenue Streams                                                   | 7. Cost Structure                                                            |
| :------------------------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Ad Revenue**: YouTube/TikTok (Low Margin / Viral-focused).         | **Infrastructure**: Tokyo electricity costs, AWS/Vercel, and AI API tokens.  |
| **Memberships**: Patreon/Discord (Medium Margin / Engagement-based). | **Human Capital**: Founder and engineer time, specialized asset outsourcing. |
| **Product Sales**: Steam Galgames (High Margin / Scalable).          | **R&D**: LoRA training cycles and workflow optimization.                     |

---

### 8-9. Moat & Metrics

| 9. Unfair Advantage (Moat)                                                                      | 8. Key Metrics                                                               |
| :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **10-GPU Farm**: On-premise compute power in Tokyo, reducing marginal experiment costs to zero. | **RPM (Revenue Per Minute)**: Aiming for > $30 (covering the $15 cost).      |
| **Full-Stack Ownership**: Vertical integration of Code (Rust), Models (LoRA), and Script (IP).  | **WVR (Wishlist/Visits Rate)**: 5% or higher conversion rate on Steam pages. |
